About Me

Hi! My name is Zolal Ayazpour. I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow based at Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics in Cambridge, Massachusetts. I completed my Ph.D. in Environmental Engineering at the State University of New York at Buffalo in May 2025. I was also a Graduate Visiting Student and a Predoctoral Research Fellow at Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics from 2020 to 2025. My research centers on studying the chemistry and physics of the Earth’s atmosphere, with a particular focus on satellite-based observations. I develop algorithms to retrieve trace gas column densities and estimate emissions of short-lived air pollutants. I am also interested in applying data-driven and machine learning methods to advance understanding of atmospheric features and air pollution.

Research

Here is a list of my main research projects:

o Retrieving trace gas Level-2 column amounts from Level-1B satellite observations

o Project: Ozone Monitoring Instrument (OMI) collection 4 formaldehyde retrieval and validation

o Estimating Level-4 emissions using Level-2/3 satellite data products

o Project: Evaluation of the Directional Derivative Approach (DDA) for satellite-based emission estimation using chemical transport model simulation of nitrogen oxides

o Project: Estimation of agricultural nitrogen oxides emissions observed by the Tropospheric Emissions: Monitoring of Pollution (TEMPO) satellite instrument using a deep learning model

o Generating a spatially complete atmospheric product from sparse observations using a machine learning approach

o Project: Estimation of a spatially complete planetary boundary layer height product over the contiguous United States
